“... And Bread Became A Dream” *

by On Yee Lo in collaboration with Handmade Bakery, Slaithwaite.

Winter Process residency exhibition at Dai Hall, Huddersfield.

5 - 15 December 2023

Organised by ame C.I.C.



- ONE -

  • 1. Who are you and how are you today?

    I’m Kirsty, foodie arty mate, who is doing pretty well today thanks

  • 2. “Where did you come from / how did you arrive?”

    I drove over to Tom’s house with Hazel the dachshund.

  • 3. Who did you share the bread with? What did you eat it with? And why?

    I ate the bread with Onyee and an intriguing Irish woman whose name I have forgotten. I helped Onyee make the makeshift cooking equipment, so was very excited to try the bread. Onyee provided a lunch of 3 curries to accompany the bread.

  • 4. When was the last time you made the same dish that you ate the bread with, and who did you eat it with?

    I ate some of these curries with Onyee the week before when we were making the equipment.

  • 5. Can you share the recipe?

    The recipe is super simple as you just go the Londis in StokenNewingont and buy it! Made by the mum of the shop, really good.

  • 6. “what’s in the larder?”

    Tins, pulses, seeds, nuts, grains. Ingredients for a cooking adventure.

  • 7. When was the last time you felt hungry? What did you crave for?

    All the time, but last time just now. I ate some leftovers of stuffed tinned dolmades and porchetta, but I’m actually craving what I am slow cooking for dinner, wholesome vegetable tea and roast pumpkin. Sole food in cold weather.

  • 8. When and what was the last thing you traded? What was it for? And who did you trade with?

    I traded space for a meal. I gave the meal to my partner and a friend and allowed 2 amazing women to use my bar for a pop up, I helped them and worked the shift.

  • 9. Anyone in town you wish to know personally?

    Ralph Ruggoff, curator of the Hayward. He really pushes great shows through that sometimes much that space cot the limit.

  • 10. “what makes a party?”

    A space perfect size for the number of people, music, warm low lighting, generous people.

  • 11. “what kind of a job is a job without pay?”

    Small business ownership or Slavery.

  • 12. “What are the consequences of silence?”

    Cancer, in our bodies and in society, a killer.

- TWO -

  • 1. Who are you and how are you today?

    I'm Oliver, a Baker from the handmade bakery. I'm well, but a little tired, normal for an early riser

  • 2. “Where did you come from / how did you arrive?”

    I came from home and drove here

  • 3. Who did you share the bread with? What did you eat it with? And why?

    I ate it alone, I dipped it into red pepper hummus. Because it is the best accompaniment for flat bread in my opinion

  • 4. When was the last time you made the same dish that you ate the bread with, and who did you eat it with?

    Last year, for my mum and sister I made hummus, falafel and chicken swarma which we ate with fresh salads in the garden.

  • 5. Can you share the recipe?

    Flour, water, salt, yeast

  • 6. “what’s in the larder?”

    I don't have one

  • 7. When was the last time you felt hungry? What did you crave for?

    This lunch time at work I was really craving a full English fry-up breakfast

  • 8. When and what was the last thing you traded? What was it for? And who did you trade with?

    Last week I traded a sandwich for some Hessian coffee sacks to make cushions out of.

  • 9. Anyone in town you wish to know personally?

    Not that I can think of. I always try to be open to creating new relationships though

  • 10. “what makes a party?”

    If a group of people, strangers or friends gather with the intention of having fun or sharing and experience together. Always Better with food and music

  • 11. “what kind of a job is a job without pay?”

    A volunteer, passion project or a slave

  • 12. “What are the consequences of silence?”

    People listen more and you hear things you can't when you make noise


2, 12 - Taken from “Twelve Questions”, from the book “The Vertical Interrogation of Strangers” (2001) by Bhanu Kapil

6, 9, 10, 11 - Taken from “Ample Food for Stupid Thought” (1965) by Robert Filliou

* “He ate what we had for two days, and bread became a dream.”, Photo caption by Samar Abu Elouf, photojournalist based in Gaza, on IG on 9 November 2023
